Your First Garden

Should it be vegetable, flowers or both.

The most beautiful and productive gardens take planning. How much space do I have?
Should I plant veggies, shrubberies, flowers? Take a walk around the area you would like
to plant and then sit down with a pencil and paper. Sketch out your area first. How would
you like to edge it? They are many ways to edge a garden; rubber strip, brick or pressure
treated lumber are a few options.

Bring in some gardening soil to an equal part of dehydrated cow manure. Then add enough
peet moss to fluff the soil up a bit.

If planting flowers, do some research about perennials and annuals. What zone do they grow
best in? Planting zone map can be found online. Do the plants need sun or shade? If flowers,
what time of year do they blossom and for how long? Will they be tall or low to the grown?
Knowing this will allow you to plant in a way to not block your view of different plants
as they bloom.

Vegetable may vine and need support trellises. Tomato cages may be purchased or build.

A well thought plan will produce a pleasurable viewing with bountiful results. So joyfully reap
what you so.
